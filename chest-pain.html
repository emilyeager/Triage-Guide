Chest Pain

← Back to Triage Question Guides

1. PQRST pain assessment

Provoking/palliating features

  • Does it get worse on mobilisation?
  • Does it get worse on inspiration/expiration?
  • Does it get worse post-eating?

Quality

  • Is the quality sharp, dull, pressure, throbbing etc.?

Radiation

  • Does the pain radiate to the back, shoulder, abdomen etc.?

Severity

  • Rate 0–10?
  • Does the patient appear distressed? ie: clutching chest, bent over etc.

Time

  • When did the pain start?
  • Is the pain constant or intermittent?
  • Did the pain start suddenly or gradually?

2. Associated symptoms

A: Patent vs compromised? SpO2?
B: SOB? RR?
C: HR? Pale? PWD? Diaphoretic? Capillary refill? Radial pulse strong vs thready? BP if appears symptomatic?
D: Temperature? GCS? Infective symptoms such as cough, cold, runny nose, fever, N/V/D?

3. Determine if likely cardiac/non-cardiac

  • MSK: Trauma to chest?
  • Reflux: Worse post-eating?
  • Pleuritic: Worse on inspiration?
  • Respiratory: Consider pneumonia, PE, pneumothorax
